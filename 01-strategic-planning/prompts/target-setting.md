# Prompt: Conditional Target Setting for Engineering Orgs

## When to use
When leadership demands committed targets but:
- Baseline data does not exist yet
- Teams have uneven control over their delivery
- The person setting targets cannot afford to own a miss alone
- A skip-level or senior stakeholder has a history of not 
  backing the team when things go wrong

## The core principle
Targets should be conditional and co-owned — not unilateral promises.
Every target needs a named condition. If the condition is not met 
by the organisation, the target adjusts. Refinement is formal, 
documented, and requires sign-off from both sides.

## The two-level structure

**Level 1 - Fixed Process Commitments**
What you will do regardless of outcome.
Never changes. Unconditional.
Examples: measurement in place by date X, reviews held every quarter,
tools deployed by date Y.

**Level 2 - Directional Targets**
The improvement direction you are committing to.
Starts as a range. Tightens each quarter as evidence accumulates.
Both upward and downward refinement valid.
All changes require joint sign-off.

## The protective framing
"We commit to a direction, measure every quarter, and refine together 
based on evidence. Every target has a named condition. If those 
conditions are not met, the target adjusts. That is not hedging —
that is how a mature organisation manages accountability."

## The mutual accountability table
Always present targets in two columns:
- What engineering commits to
- What the organisation commits to

If leadership accepts the targets but refuses the conditions,
that is your signal. Document it.

## What to document after every review
- What the data showed
- What caused variance
- What was refined and why
- What changes next period
- Sign-off from both engineering and leadership
- Email summary within 24 hours

## Prompt template for LLM

Use this to get an LLM to help you build conditional targets
for any org-wide initiative:

---

You are an enterprise engineering strategy advisor.
I need to set targets for [INITIATIVE] in a [SIZE] engineer 
organisation. Help me build conditional, co-owned targets that
I can commit to without carrying all the risk alone.

Context:
- What the initiative is: [DESCRIBE]
- What data I have now: [BASELINE STATUS]
- What data I am missing: [GAPS]
- Who sets direction but may not back the team: [STAKEHOLDER]
- What is outside my team's control: [CONSTRAINTS]
- What the organisation needs to provide: [DEPENDENCIES]

Build me:
1. Three to five directional targets with named conditions
2. A mutual accountability table
3. A quarterly refinement process
4. The protective framing to present this to leadership

Be direct. Find loopholes. Tell me what will be challenged.
